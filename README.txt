Data on opioid- and stimulant-related harms in Canada
======================================

The overdose crisis has affected every part of the country, but there are clear differences in rates and numbers across provinces and territories. 

The national-level data contained in this report are based on available data reported as of February 11, 2021.

Authors: Public Health Agency of Canada and provincial and territorial (PT) offices of Chief Coroners and Chief Medical Examiners as well as PT public health and health partners and Emergency Medical Services data providers. Canadian Institute for Health Information (CIHI) collected and provided the data used for reporting opioid-related poisoning hospitalizations.


Files
-----

* The TechnicalNotes_Deaths.pdf file contains all the considerations and limitations of the mortality data presented on the Public Health Infobase platform.

* The TechnicalNotes_Hospitalizations.pdf file contains all the considerations and limitations of the hospitalization data presented on the Public Health Infobase platform.

* The TechnicalNotes_EMS.pdf file contains all the considerations and limitations of the EMS data presented on the Public Health Infobase platform.

* The SubstanceHarmsData.csv contains opioid- and stimulant-related harms (mortality, hospitalizations, emergency medical services) data in Canada by province and territory.
